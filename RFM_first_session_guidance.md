# Reasoning-First Methodology — First Session Guidance
`v0.0.0.3` // `first_session_guidance` // [living]

---

## Before you begin

You are about to produce your first RFM reasoning document. A few things to hold before you start.

**Bring the right problem.** Choose something genuine — a decision or practice where the reasoning actually matters to you. Not too large: a problem you already know something about is better than one you are still discovering. The session sharpens the problem. It cannot manufacture one.

**Protect the time.** Set aside roughly two hours of uninterrupted, focused time. This session is more cognitively demanding than it looks. If you are depleted or distracted, reschedule. The session is designed to succeed with an engaged mind. It is not designed to compensate for an absent one.

**Have a place to save your work.** The session produces a reasoning document section by section. After each section is confirmed, the LLM will present a clean markdown block of everything drafted so far. Copy it somewhere you control — a notes app, a document, anywhere — before continuing. Do not wait until the end of the session.

**The document you produce will be imperfect.** That is correct and expected. RFM documents are living artifacts — the first version is a starting point, not a deliverable. You are beginning a reasoning practice, not producing an authoritative artifact.

---

## What to expect from the session

The LLM working with you is a co-author, not an assistant. It will ask questions more than it supplies answers. It will push back when your thinking is loose. It will not fill sections on your behalf. It will also challenge claims that outrun their evidence — that is not the same as filling sections, and both are part of how the session works.

The session uses suggested formatting conventions for each section — the LLM will introduce them at the right moment. You do not need to know them in advance.

This can feel unfamiliar. It is working correctly when it does.

The session is yours to pace. You decide when a section has been reasoned through honestly enough to move forward. The LLM will not rush you.

After the Landscape section, the session pauses and returns to what you wrote earlier. Landscape research changes things — that revision loop is part of the design, not a detour.

---

## The one failure mode to watch for

As the session lengthens, the LLM may drift from asking questions toward producing answers. When responses feel like answers rather than questions, name it directly:

*"You are in tool mode, not co-author mode."*

At each section boundary, before moving forward, say:

*"Hold your co-author role."*

These are not politeness rituals. They are the recalibration moves that keep the session honest.

---

## When you are ready

Open a new session with the traveling prompt as system prompt. Paste the guided drafting prompt as your opening message. Then describe the problem you are bringing — as clearly or as roughly as you have it. The session will do the rest.
